ABOUT THE PROJECT

Choice of two Manifestos to Build a Knowledge Base

A manifesto is a powerful declaratory mean aimed to publicly express views and scopes of, generally, artistic or political nature. We were fascinated by the idea of attempting a comparison between two models of a text as such.

After some research, we decided to work on the 'Manifesto del Futurismo' (1909) and on the 'Manifesto dei Pittori Futuristi' (1911).

(Manual) Extraction of the Concepts

For each Manifesto, we manually isolated a section arranged by the author(s) in numbered points, as the quasi-symmetrical structure of this parts was adequate for the comparison. Furthermore, we identified in these lines the textual sites where the meaning was most robustly conveyed.

As a starting point, we underlined and manually isolated significant lexical terms and short excerpts for each Manifesto. The brainstorming following this phase resulted in the creation of a terms linking - a sort of parallel of the Semantic Annotation procedures. At this stage, we started to see the relations among the extracted terms.

This process allowed us to pinpoint the main concepts contained in each text. Thanks to the two wordmaps we not only gained with-text knowledge, but also a first tool to identify cross-texts relations.
